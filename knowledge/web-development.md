# Web Development — Sales Knowledge Base

## What We Offer
Custom websites and web applications. We work with React, Next.js, and Node.js for web apps, and WordPress / Webflow for content sites. We do NOT do mobile apps (separate service), and we don't maintain legacy PHP/jQuery codebases unless scoped separately.

## Ideal Customer Profile
- Has a clear idea of what they need (or open to a discovery workshop at $500)
- Budget matches the scope they're describing
- Has content ready (or willing to pay for copywriting — we can refer)
- Has a decision maker available for weekly check-ins
- Realistic timeline expectations (not "I need it in 2 weeks" for a complex app)

## Disqualifiers — Politely Decline If:
- Budget under $2,000 (refer to DIY tools like Squarespace / Wix)
- Wants to "just tweak" an existing site they built themselves with no documentation
- Expects unlimited revisions or scope changes mid-project
- No clear brief — and not willing to do a paid discovery session
- Wants to own the code but not pay for a proper handover

## Key Qualifying Questions to Ask
1. What do you need the website to do — inform visitors, generate leads, sell products, or run as a web app?
2. Do you have an existing site? Are you rebuilding or starting from scratch?
3. What's your timeline — is there a hard launch date (event, product launch)?
4. What's your budget range for this project?
5. Who's the decision maker, and are they involved in this conversation?

## Scoring Guide
- **HOT**: Clear scope, budget $5,000+, hard deadline, decision maker engaged, wants to start within 2–4 weeks
- **WARM**: Interested, scope or budget vague, timeline flexible, may need a discovery session first
- **COLD**: Budget under $2,000, just "exploring", no clear brief, or trying to get free advice

## Common Objections & Responses
- *"Can't I just use Wix/Squarespace?"* → "Absolutely — for a basic presence, those work fine. We're the right fit when you need custom functionality, integrations, or want to scale beyond what templates allow."
- *"Why so expensive?"* → "You're paying for a custom solution, not a template. Our projects include discovery, design, development, testing, and handover. A cheap site often costs more in fixes later."
- *"How long will it take?"* → "For a business website, typically 5–8 weeks. Rushing timelines usually means rushed QA — we'd rather set a realistic date and hit it."
